About the lab

The primary objective of this lab is to calculate the landslide susceptibility maps and indicators of landslide hazard exposure for the watersheds in the Lombardy region of Italy. The results of the analysis will be presented through a website that includes WebGIS content. This goal will be achieved by utilizing population map and environmental factors such as landslide inventory, DEM.

About the area

Grigna Settentrionale, the highest peak in the Gruppo delle Grigne, is situated between Lago di Como and la Valsassina. This mountain is predominantly made of sedimentary rock and is characterized by its karst topography. In the summer, warm and moist air currents from the clear plains flow north towards the Alps, frequently causing thunderstorms over the mountain. Conversely, in winter, winds blow the clouds away from the peaks, moving them towards the southern plains.

Study Area

The Grigna Settentrionale area is prone to landslides primarily due to the weathering and erosion of limestone layers, complex geological structures and fault lines, high rainfall and snowmelt, steep slopes and valley terrains, and environmental pressures from human activities such as road construction and tourism development.
