workflow

This section will present the workflow applied to obtain the Landslide Susceptibility Map and an indicator of landslide hazard exposure.

STEP 1

Data preprocessing for susceptibility mapping

Clipping the data, conducting DTM analysis, generating training and testing points, and sampling the resulting layers.

step 2

Susceptibility map generation

Conduct a random forest classification and transform the of the classifications confidence into class probability.

step 3 & Step 4

Exposure assessment

Preprocess the population map data and calculate the population counts within each susceptibility class.

step 5

Develop a website and WebGIS

Develop a website that explain what the project is about, the technologies and data used, the results achieved, and a WebGIS displaying a web map with the produced layers.

profile curvature

Represents the slope curvature on the profile side which affect landslide creation.

After determining the No Landslide Zones (NLZ) based on slope, we assigned a hazard value of 1 to the NLZ and a value of 2 to the Landslide Inventory (LS). We then randomly created 1000 points, distributing 700 points for training and 300 points for testing, evenly across the NLZ and LS regions.

Step 2 - Susceptibility map generation

Utilize the training points to conduct a random forest classification on all environmental factors. Convert the classification confidence to class probability, and then validate the output.

Step 3 & 4 - Data preprocessing and Exposure assessment

For the 2020 WorldPop - Population Counts data of Italy, start by reprojecting and clipping. Then, reclassify the susceptibility map into four categories: low, medium, high, and very high. Finally, resample the reclassified susceptibility raster map to match the extent and resolution of the clipped population raster dataset.
